Getting Started
What is PhishGuard AI?
PhishGuard AI is a machine-learning powered URL scanner that detects phishing websites. It uses a Random Forest classifier trained on 30+ URL features to determine whether a link is legitimate or a phishing attempt — all in under one second.
Is PhishGuard AI free to use?
Yes, entirely free. Create an account with your email (or continue with Google), and you get unlimited URL scans, full history tracking, and detailed analysis reports — no subscription required.
Do I need technical knowledge to use it?
Not at all. Simply paste a URL and press Scan. The results are presented in plain English, and every warning signal is explained so you can understand exactly why a URL was flagged.
URL Scanner
What types of URLs can I scan?
Any URL — http:// or https://. This includes links from emails, WhatsApp messages, SMS, social media posts, QR codes, or any website you've visited. If you're unsure whether a link is safe, scan it first.
How fast is the analysis?
Typically under 500 milliseconds. The model runs locally on the server — there are no external API calls, so results are near-instant regardless of your internet speed.
What does "confidence" mean?
Confidence shows how certain the model is about its verdict, expressed as a percentage. For example, 99% confidence means 198 out of 200 decision trees in our Random Forest agreed on the classification. Higher confidence = stronger signal.
Can it detect all phishing sites?
Our model achieves 100% accuracy on our test dataset, but no system is perfect. Very new phishing domains that mimic legitimate sites perfectly may occasionally pass. Always use PhishGuard AI as a first line of defence — not the only one. If you're in doubt, don't click.
Why are my past URLs saved?
Your scan history is saved to your dashboard so you can review past results, re-read the explanations, or track patterns. You can delete any record at any time from your dashboard. Your history is private and only visible to you.
Account
How do I change my password?
Go to My Account from the top navigation or user menu, then navigate to the Security tab. Enter your current password, then choose a new one. Changes take effect immediately.
Can I delete my account?
Yes. From My Account → Danger Zone you can permanently delete your account along with all associated scan history. This action is irreversible, so please be certain before proceeding.
What happens when I sign in with Google?
Google OAuth lets you create and sign into your PhishGuard AI account using your existing Google credentials. We only receive your name and email address. We do not access your Gmail, Drive, or any other Google data.
Security & Privacy
Is my data safe?
Passwords are stored as SHA-256 hashes — never in plain text. Your scan history is stored in a private SQLite database and is only accessible to your account. We do not sell or share your data with third parties.
Does scanning a URL visit the website?
No. PhishGuard AI analyses the URL string itself — it does not make any HTTP request to the target website. You are never exposed to potentially malicious content during a scan.
The AI Model
What is a Random Forest classifier?
A Random Forest is an ensemble machine learning algorithm that builds many decision trees during training and combines their outputs for a final prediction. Each tree votes on the classification, and the majority wins. This makes it highly accurate and resistant to overfitting.
What features does the model use?
The model analyses 30+ features including: URL length, domain length, number of dots/hyphens, HTTPS presence, IP address in URL, URL shortener detection, suspicious TLD (.tk, .ml, etc.), suspicious keywords, brand impersonation, subdomain depth, hex encoding, digit ratio, special character count, and more.
How was the model trained?
The model was trained on a balanced dataset of 1,600 samples (800 legitimate, 800 phishing) drawn from known phishing patterns and real websites. It uses 200 decision trees and was validated with an 80/20 train-test split, achieving 100% accuracy on held-out data.
Common Phishing Signs
  • ● IP address instead of domain name
  • ● Suspicious TLDs like .tk, .ml, .ga
  • ● Brand name in subdomain (not root)
  • ● Keywords: "verify", "secure", "login"
  • ● No HTTPS encryption
  • ● Very long or cluttered URLs
  • ● URL shorteners hiding destination
  • ● @ symbol in the URL path
